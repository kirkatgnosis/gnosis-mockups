Question 11 · your answer B · correct

One of the main points the author seeks to make is that American citizens:

  • Acannot understand government because they read too much media.interpretation
  • ∴ B have little chance of improving government because they do not understand its important details.
  • Ccan control technical decisions through elections.part true, part not
  • Donce had a responsive government before television.interpretation

Why B is correct. Lines 58-61 state directly that voters are "unable to ... tell the rascals how to do their jobs better" because "they themselves do not understand the technical questions."

Citation feedback

Lines 58-61 are exactly right. This is the thesis of the passage.

Question 13 · you chose B · answer A

The 1978 tax seminar is used to make the point that some governmental issues are:

  • ∴ A so technical that not even career civil servants can understand them.
  • B so technical that only career civil servants can understand them. reverse relationship
  • Cmore technical than before the Tax Reform Act.interpretation
  • Dtoo technical for anyone but an IRS tax lawyer.reverse relationship

Reverse relationship. The passage says even the IRS lawyers couldn't understand it. This choice flips "not even they can" into "only they can." One word ("only") reverses the entire meaning.

Citation feedback

The strongest evidence is lines 62-68. Lines 62-65 state the point; lines 65-68 illustrate it with the tax seminar example.

Question 14 · you chose J · answer F

In naming indifference a central fact of political life (line 87), the author most likely means citizens are:

  • ∴ F not concerned about the technical but important details of government.
  • Gcompletely taken in by the myth of a responsive government.exact words, wrong context
  • Hmore responsive to elected officials than to bureaucrats.interpretation
  • J unwilling to grant legitimacy unless government is elected. reverse relationship

Reverse relationship, again. The passage says citizens do grant legitimacy through the myth (lines 84-85). This choice says they won't grant it. Opposite direction. The second time this trap caught you.

Citation feedback

The best evidence is lines 42-44, where the author says the electorate does "not particularly care" about technical issues. That's what "indifference" means here.
